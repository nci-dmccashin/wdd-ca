Recycling Information Centre

Plastic Pollution

Plastic pollution occurs when plastic ends up in our environment instead of being reused or recycled correctly. Due to the fact plastic typically takes hundreds of years to break down, it quickly builds up in oceans, rivers and soil. This harms our wildlife, damages ecosystems and causes long-term environmental damage. This is only worsened with the more single use plastics we produce.

Litter

Litter is waste that is thrown or left on the ground instead of being thrown out. Even the smallest bits such as wrappers, bottles or cigarette butts can cause big environmental damage. Litter damages communities and can have disastrous impacts on wildlife and the environment. The impact of litter can be stopped by throwing away waste correctly.

Health Impacts

Pollution and the incorrect disposal of waste can have serious health impacts on the communities it effects. Chemicals from materials such as plastic can leach into groundwater as they break down. This in turn causes many types of cancer as well as other diseases. The incorrect disposal of waste can attract rodents and other pests that carry disease.

The Return Scheme

The deposit return scheme encourages the popper disposal of single use plastic bottles by charging a deposit that is returned when the bottle is return. This scheme encourages the popper disposal of plastic bottles which is hugely beneficial at reducing plastic waste as well as reducing litter. 3/4 of eligible bottles are returned in Ireland.

Global Warming

Global warming is the warming of our climate. It is a direct function of increased carbon emissions. As we release more carbon dioxide into our atmosphere, more heat is trapped in our atmosphere. This increases the temperature of our climate. This can cause extreme weather such as wildfires, hurricanes, floods, or extreme heat. This can be devastating for communities, especially communities that already deal with a very hot climate.
